The 5 Layers of the Ocean (Depth Zones)



The ocean changes drastically as you dive deeper due to the rapid loss of sunlight and massive increases in water pressure. Scientists divide the water column into five primary ecological zones: Sunlight Zone (Epipelagic) Depth: Surface down to 200 meters (656 feet) Characteristics: This layer absorbs nearly all available sunlight. Because of this, it is where 90% of all marine life lives. It hosts coral reefs, marine plants, and photosynthetic organisms like phytoplankton, which produce more than half of the world's oxygen.
